Chapter One

Introduction

Background of the study

  • Web3 is the new version of the internet that runs on blockchain — letting people own and control their own data and money directly, without one company in the middle.
  • Examples include cryptocurrency, NFTs, and apps that don't need a bank or a big company as an intermediary.
  • Many young Nigerians have started using Web3, driven by a large youth population, widespread smartphone use, and a naira that keeps losing value.
  • As smartphone and internet access expand, adoption is expected to deepen, positioning Nigeria as a leading African hub for crypto and blockchain activity.

Why Nigeria?

  • Largest youth population in Africa
  • High smartphone & mobile internet use
  • Naira depreciation driving alternatives

Chapter Two

Project Overview

2.1

What is Web3

Web3 is the new version of the internet built on blockchain. Unlike Web2 platforms (Facebook, Google, bank apps), no single company controls it.

Examples:

  • Bitcoin & Ethereum
  • Crypto wallets (MetaMask)
  • NFTs

2.2

Web3 Developers in Nigeria

Beyond trading crypto, Nigeria has many people building Web3 projects, apps, wallets, and blockchain tools.

Some work for Nigerian startups; others work remotely for foreign companies for better pay and opportunity.

Growth is expected as more universities add blockchain and smart contract development to their curricula, expanding the local talent pool.

Chapter Three

Methodology & Technical Feasibility

3.1 — Factors shaping whether Web3 can realistically work in Nigeria

Infrastructure Gaps

Inconsistent electricity and internet disrupt access and slow development work.

Limited Developer Pool

A growing but still limited pool of skilled blockchain developers in the country.

Mobile Accessibility

Tools like MetaMask and Ethereum are reachable via smartphones and basic internet.

Regulatory Uncertainty

Absence of clear government regulation creates risk for financial/blockchain products.

Verdict: Web3 development is technically feasible on a small-to-medium scale today — nationwide adoption depends on infrastructure, talent, and regulatory progress.

Chapter Three (continued)

Data Collection & Analysis

Method of Data Collection

A questionnaire was created with simple questions about Web3 challenges and opportunities in Nigeria. It was shared online and answered by selected respondents.

Chapter Four

Advantages of Web3 in Nigeria

Financial Inclusion

Crypto wallets give millions of unbanked Nigerians access to financial services.

Hedge Against Inflation

As the naira loses value, more Nigerians turn to crypto to preserve savings.

Job & Income Opportunities

New income streams through trading, freelance development, and remote jobs.

Faster, Cheaper Transactions

Crypto enables quicker, lower-cost international transfers than traditional banks.

Innovation & Skill Growth

Developers learn smart contracts and blockchain programming, raising the tech profile.

Entrepreneurship

Opens doors for Nigerians to launch blockchain-based startups and businesses.

Chapter Four (continued)

Disadvantages of Web3 in Nigeria

Regulatory Uncertainty

Lack of clear laws makes it risky for developers and discourages institutional investment.

Scams & Fraud

Many Nigerians have lost money to fake platforms, Ponzi schemes, and fraudulent airdrops.

Poor Infrastructure

Unstable electricity and internet make reliable building and access difficult.

Developer Brain Drain

Many skilled developers leave or work exclusively for foreign companies.

Limited Funding

Local startups struggle to access capital compared to more developed crypto markets.

Low Public Education

Many Nigerians don't fully understand Web3, leaving them vulnerable to scams.

Chapter Five

Summary & Recommendations

Most Nigerians surveyed are aware of Web3 and believe it has a good future — but unclear laws, poor electricity and internet, and low funding are slowing growth.

  • Government to create clear, simple laws for crypto and blockchain.

  • Improve electricity and internet services to support developers.

  • Increase funding and support for local Web3 startups.

  • Educate the public on how to spot and avoid crypto scams.

  • Introduce crypto and Web3 basics into school curricula.

Conclusion

Web3 in Nigeria has strong potential because of the young, tech-savvy population and high interest in crypto. If the challenges identified in this study are addressed, adoption will likely accelerate, more local blockchain startups will emerge, and Nigeria will be better positioned to become a leading Web3 hub in Africa.
